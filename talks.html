    (1 credit course) DePaul College of Law, 2018, 2019

    Instruction & Training

    General legal research support to the College of Law including Legal Analysis Research and Communication (LARC) Program, Law Journals, Faculty, and the general student body at the point of need. Services include:

    • Providing library orientation sessions during the first-year law students’ (1L) orientation week to introduce them to library services and register them for primary legal databases, Lexis Advance, WestLaw, Bloomberg Law, and CALI;
    • Providing research instruction sessions or workshops that provide law students with an introduction to legal research and assist them with learning about certain specifics of the American legal system such as court structure, jurisdiction, and hierarchy of authority; primary and secondary legal resources; how to analyze a fact pattern; how to identify the appropriate resources for their information need; how to become effective at navigating databases and utilizing its browsing features; and how to utilize research strategies such as generating search terms and keyword searching and create research plans to tackle their legal issue. Sessions for journals are typically tailored to the cite checking process and will place emphasis on sources and services that conform to rules and standards that the journal must adhere to;
    • Creating materials such as tutorials or modules for faculty or LARC program as requested that supplement course curriculum topics being covered in courses at the time of the sessions. These materials are often embedded directly into the courses D2L pages for students to review as instructed by the faculty.
